Mother Teresa

Roman Catholic saint and Nobel laureate known for her missionary work with the poor in India

This photo, taken in December 1980, shows Mother Teresa with a group of children who she looked after in Kolkata, India

Here's a time line of Mother Teresa's life:

  • 1910 - Born as AnjezÃ« Gonxhe Bojaxhiu in Skopje, Ottoman Empire (present-day North Macedonia)
  • 1928 - At the age of 18, she leaves home to join the Sisters of Loreto in Ireland
  • 1929 - She arrives in India and begins her novitiate in Darjeeling
  • 1931 - Takes her first vows as a nun and chooses the name Sister Mary Teresa
  • 1946 - While traveling by train to Darjeeling for a retreat, she receives what she describes as a "call within a call" from Jesus to serve the poorest of the poor
  • 1948 - Receives permission to leave the Sisters of Loreto and starts the Missionaries of Charity, initially focused on caring for the sick and dying in the streets of Kolkata
  • 1950 - Establishes the first Home for the Dying in Kolkata, called Nirmal Hriday (Pure Heart)
  • 1952 - Opens the first orphanage in Kolkata under the Missionaries of Charity
  • 1957 - Establishes the Missionaries of Charity Brothers, a contemplative branch of the Sisters
  • 1971 - Receives the inaugural Pope John XXIII Peace Prize for her humanitarian work
  • 1979 - Awarded the Nobel Peace Prize for her efforts to help the poor
  • 1997 - Suffers from declining health and steps down as head of the Missionaries of Charity
  • 1997 - Dies on September 5th in Kolkata, India, at the age of 87
  • 2016 - Canonized as Saint Teresa of Calcutta by Pope Francis on September 4th

Throughout her life, Mother Teresa dedicated herself to serving the poorest of the poor, embodying compassion and selflessness. Her work continues to inspire millions around the world.
